Risk Profile — "How do they handle uncertainty?"
When facing an uncertain outcome, does {{ player.name }} play it safe, play the odds, or swing for the fences? This affects how they respond to challenges from other players.
{{ form.risk_profile }}
🛡 Risk Averse
Plays it safe. Would rather accept a smaller guaranteed gain than risk a loss. Avoids confrontation unless the odds are strongly in their favor.

Example: A sales rep who'd rather close at a lower price than risk losing the sale entirely.
⚖️ Risk Neutral
Plays the odds. Weighs potential gains and losses equally. Will challenge when the expected value is positive, regardless of variance.

Example: A data-driven buyer who calculates their BATNA and negotiates accordingly. Most common profile.
🎲 Risk Acceptant
Swings for the fences. Willing to accept a higher chance of loss for the possibility of a big win. Challenges aggressively even when odds are uncertain.

Example: A startup founder making bold demands in an acquisition negotiation — they'll walk away from a good deal if they think a great one is possible.
